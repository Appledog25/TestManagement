A Guide to helping students get help
Student in distress

College students, just like many other adults, sometimes experience distress and associated difficulties. An array of resources are available to assure that students get the assistance they require to help them stay healthy, well, and academically successful.

Student Counseling Services(SCS)

+91-8178*****
Appropriate referrals to DoD include students:

  • anticipating or returning from an extended absence
  • considering withdrawal from the current semester
  • feeling overwhelmed by a family or personal emergency
  • having a concern about a classroom issue

    • Students being disruptive

    When students disrupt the University community or potentially violate ISUs Code of Student Conduct or other university policies, it is important that faculty and staff involve Student Conduct and Conflict Resolution, a unit of the Dean of Students Office.

    Student Conduct and Conflict Resolution (SCCR)

    +91-8888888888
    Appropriate referrals to SCCR include students exhibiting:

  • disruption to the University community
  • academic integrity allegations
  • violation of university policy and/or procedures

    • Emergencies

    Circumstances that threaten the safety of a student or others—or any other unsafe conditions—should be immediately reported to the Illinois State University Police Department.
    Faculty and staff should immediately contact ISUPD when a student:

  • threatens immediate harm to self and/or others
  • acts in a threatening or frightening manner
  • refuses to leave a classroom after being instructed to leave
  • initiates a threat or a bomb scare

    • We hope this resource assists faculty and staff in supporting our students and cultivating a safe university community. When deciding whether to report an incident or issue, you are welcome to contact professionals from any of these offices to receive consultation and advice. Supporting students and protecting our campus is everyones responsibility. Your partnership in building and sustaining a caring Redbird community is appreciated.

    Note: Regardless of the situation, contact any of the offices for support or information.
